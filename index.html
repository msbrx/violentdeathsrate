Violent Death Disproportionately High in Black Populations

By Moses Bustos
December 16th, 2020

Civil unrest erupted after the death of George Floyd, who died under police custody in Minneapolis, MN on May 25th, 2020. Protests against systemic racism condemned police violence inflicted on black bodies. Such violence is mirrored in examples of racism and lack of diversity seen in the media today.

NYC data regarding leading causes of death illuminate how natural causes of death like heart failure is highest among whites, while assault/homicide is highest among Blacks.

Jim Debarros, former vice president of creative at MTV, says “The media reflects and amplifies the violence in black and brown communities. Lack of trust in government, authority and systems mean black and brown people are less inclined to seek professional assistance for seemingly minor ailments that would lead to preventative measures and better health choices.” That lack of choices is denounced as racial injustice and questions prevalent use of deadly force in reality and fiction.

Sources: NYC OPEN DATA
